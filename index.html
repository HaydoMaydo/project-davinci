About Project DaVinci CubeSat

Our Mission

Project DaVinci is a team of high school students in rural North Idaho working with the community’s best mentors to launch a SATELLITE! As the only high school chosen by NASA to launch in 2017, the team is looking to connect everyone with space again. “We are determined to reignite the magic of the Apollo days,” said team member Joe Broder. The team has recognized the lack of enthusiasm shown towards space in the recent years and aims to use tools such as social media and virtual reality to give students and individuals alike a personal connection with space.

Satellite Overview

Project DaVinci's mission is to light up minds around the world using a satellite that anyone can use. The satellite will be a 3U, or three unit, satellite that is 4x4x12 inches. This satellite will have the standard capability to communicate with a ground station and any receiver kits tuned to receive the correct frequency. The satellite will also be blazing the trail for internet in space with the first satellite to satellite internet communication using GlobalStar. Project DaVinci’s satellite will have the added capability to take pictures and send them back to the ground station where the team will upload them to this website for public use.

Bitcoin from Space

With our detailed instructions you could become one of the first people to receive Bitcoin from space! This is possible, because of our CubeSat's connection with the Global Star satellite system. The system enables our satellite to connect to the Internet through one of many satellites constantly circling the Earth. To experience a Bitcoin transfer from space, simply register with our website and ensure you have a Bitcoin wallet.

Our Mission
"lighting up minds around the world"

Project DaVinci is a team of high school students in rural North Idaho working with the community’s best mentors to launch a SATELLITE! As the only high school chosen by NASA to launch a CubeSat, the team is looking to connect the world to space again. “We are determined to reignite the magic of the Apollo days,” said team member Joe Broder. The team has recognized the lack of enthusiasm shown towards space in the recent years and aims to use tools such as social media and virtual reality to give students and individuals alike a personal connection with space.

An Educational Beginning
"sharing the gift of education"

The main focus of Project DaVinci is to inspire students around the world to pursue STEM education, careers, and fields. The team attends North Idaho STEM Charter Academy and has experienced what a project-based education can do. They want to share the gift of education with students from every country on Earth. Project DaVinci’s goal is to connect with at least one “partner school” from every country and one from every state in the United States. “Our goal is to get students of all ages, races, genders, religions, and nationalities involved in our project. Space is truly for everyone!” logistics lead (ret.) Paige Pence stated. Project DaVinci will give partner schools access to the satellite via radio receiver kits, and students will have complete access to free lesson plans that focus on space topics. For example, one lesson plan allows students to build mock comets and learn about the different materials comets are comprised of. Additionally, students will be granted access to an online virtual cockpit that allows users to feel as though they are controlling the satellite.

Satellite Overview

Radio Frequency Communication

From the Project DaVinci ground station, the team can control messages sent and received to and from the satellite. The team is partnering with schools around the world and, using a radio receiver dongle (left), students can receive messages from our satellite. Using specific frequencies such as 145.1 MHz (uplink) and 432.5 MHz (downlink), the team can upload information to the satellite and those with a receiver dongle can receive the information and decipher it.



Morse Code

Messages received will be in Morse code requiring students to download a translating app or translate it themselves. The satellite' pings will be messages in the form of long and short tones that make up letters in Morse Code. The messages will be set by the Project DaVinci Team at their ground station and will repeat continuously as the satellite circles the earth.



GlobalStar: Internet in Space

What if you could tweet from SPACE! The Project DaVinci satellite will allow you to accomplish that and more. The team has partnered with GlobalStar, a subscription based internet service that uses satellites to allow users to access the internet nearly everywhere on Earth. The Project DaVinci satellite will be doing something very different though—it will be connecting with GlobalStar in space! Project DaVinci is the first group to have a satellite connect with the GlobalStar Satellite in Space. With this internet connection in space, Project DaVinci’s satellite will tackle the first Bitcoin transaction in space.

Participate

As a Business:

  1. Join our Email list: You will need to register with Project DaVinci's website HERE. This will notify the team that you are interested and will allow the team to send you more information via email.
  2. Donate to Project DaVinci: Because of the project's non-profit status, Idaho residents can receive a substantial tax credit if they choose to donate to Project DaVinci. To receive this tax credit, donate HERE.
  3. Share Project DaVinci: Once you have donated, you can share Project DaVinci with everyone you know. This will encourage others in your community to support a satellite for all!




As a School:

  1. Join our Email list: You will need to complete the registration with Project DaVinci's website HERE. This will ensure that we have a record of your participation so you don't miss out on any of the fun!
  2. Order the Receiver Kit: Do you want to connect with our satellite as it travels overhead? All you need to do it order a simple Radio Receiver Dongle. This is a simple dongle that plugs directly into your computer for ease of use. You may need a separate antennae based on your location, and we will contact you directly if this is necessary to receive a signal. To learn how to receive a signal go HERE.
  3. Download and Complete Curriculum: As a team, Project DaVinci has designed a specific curriculum that can be used for a variety of age levels. These are hands on activities that will help you inspire students to be the next generation of inventors and explorers. Check out an example HERE where students can make comets!
